## Current State

The existing analytics stack was built incrementally over 5 years. It now consists of 6 disconnected tools with overlapping capabilities, no shared data model, and significant maintenance burden.

<Callout type="warn">
**Maintenance cost is growing 20% YoY** while the number of supported dashboards has plateaued. We are paying more for diminishing returns.
</Callout>

## Pain Points

| Pain Point | Impact | Affected Teams |
|------------|--------|----------------|
| No single source of truth | Conflicting metrics in exec reviews | Leadership, Finance |
| 3-day average query latency | Decisions delayed by data lag | Sales, Marketing |
| Manual ETL pipelines | 15 hrs/week of engineering time | Data Engineering |
| No self-service capability | All requests routed through DE team | All departments |

<Callout type="error">
**Critical risk**: Two of the six tools reach end-of-life in Q3 FY26. Without action, we lose access to 40% of our current dashboards.
</Callout>

## Opportunity

A modernized, consolidated platform would:

- **Reduce time-to-insight** from 3 days to under 15 minutes
- **Free up 15 hours/week** of engineering capacity
- **Enable self-service** for 200+ business users
- **Eliminate $340K/year** in redundant tool licensing

<Callout type="info">
Competitors who have completed similar migrations report **2.3x improvement** in data-driven decision velocity (Gartner, 2025).
</Callout>

## Cost of Inaction

If we do nothing:

1. Two tools go EOL in Q3, forcing emergency migration anyway
2. Maintenance burden continues growing at 20% YoY
3. Self-service analytics remains impossible
4. Engineering team stays bottlenecked on ad-hoc reporting
